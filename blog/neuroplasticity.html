Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

Also known as neural plasticity or brain plasticity, is a brain that is forever changing. The more times the brain is stimulated to learn something that you otherwise wouldn't have, makes the brain stronger and more efficient. Practicing something that's new makes it permanent in your brain. Practice Practice Practice.

When people stop practising new things, the network connecting different parts of the brain will eventually eliminate, or"prune," the connecting cells that formed the pathways.

Growth Mindset

A person with a Growth Mindset understands and believes that with extra time and effort you can get smarter. The will to learn something new is enough to stimulate the brain to continuously grow and want to learn more.

A person with a Fixed Mindset is where your mindset restricts you because of your understanding and belief's e.g. I'm not good at maths so I can't become an accountant or I'm not doing JavaScript because I'm not clever and it's too hard.

Instead a person with a Growth Mindset would say something like I may not be great at maths but I can learn or JavaScript maybe hard for me at the moment but with practice I'll get better at it.
